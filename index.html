The Tea Time Talks

The Tea Time Talks are a series of talks primarily given by the faculty and students studying Artificial Intelligence at the University of Alberta. Everyone is welcome to attend!

The talks will start June 7th and will run until late August.

Talks in the first two weeks are given by the RLAI faculty and will run on Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Fridays. In subsequent weeks, students will give talks on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. The talks run from 4:15 pm to 4:45 pm MST.

Starting June 24th, we will host a social event on Thursdays at 4:15 pm MST. Everyone is welcome to join!

The talks and the social events will be held online, via Zoom. The Zoom link will be posted here at a later date.

Talks may be recorded and subsequently posted to the Amii Intelligence YouTube channel. Please check your Zoom privacy settings prior to joining a talk.

Guidelines for Presenters

If you're planning on presenting, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Each talk is usually either an overview of a paper or a summary of the presenter's own work and is almost always accompanied by slides.
  • An abstract of your talk needs to be written up at least 24 hours prior and sent to the coordinator.
  • Each talk should start punctually at 4:15 pm and should end punctually at 4:45 pm.
  • Each talk should be 20 minutes long, with 10 minutes afterwards for questions.
  • It's your responsibility to make sure that you pass over any disruptive questions if they divert from the talk.
  • It's highly recommended, though not essential, that you release your slides after the talk.
